# Risk Register

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

Scale: likelihood and impact are `low`, `medium`, or `high`. A risk remains open until evidence closes it.

| ID    | Risk                                                                        | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation and required evidence                                                                                               | Owner phase | Status |

| R-001 | Scope escape through symlink, junction, Unicode, or case handling           | High       | High   | Canonical allowlist checks at every boundary; adversarial fixtures; no path-only identity. The M7 database opener rejects relative/non-regular/component-link paths, uses SQLite `NOFOLLOW`, and compares platform identity before/after open, but SQLite does not expose its held main-file descriptor through rusqlite; an adversarial ancestor swap-and-restore fixture plus descriptor/VFS-bound identity design remain an explicit hardening gate | M1/M5/M7 | Open |
| R-002 | Data loss or unrecoverable move/rename                                      | Medium     | High   | ADR-017 preview plus ADR-025's bounded SHA-256/root/parent/source binding, immutable requests, closed append-only states, lease and observation-only recovery protocol pass deterministic tests. Review found two unsafe assumptions: an expiring lease cannot fence a stopped live executor, and Unix leaf-name rename can move a replacement ordinary file after the final identity check. The new deterministic counterexample reproduces that wrong-inode mutation. ADR-026 resolves D-018 by rejecting general Unix Rename/Move execution rather than accepting the race; production returns unsupported before database/journal/lock/mutation and CLI/Desktop expose no Execute/recovery/Undo. No process fence is accepted or implemented; D-019 requires a packaged-private replacement-resistant crash-released fence. Windows has no handle adapter. Move, platform fault matrices, manifest refresh and user-facing Undo remain open | M5 | Mitigating |
| R-003 | Cross-volume or removable-drive interruption leaves duplicate/partial state | High       | High   | Copy-verify-commit state machine, resumable cleanup policy without permanent deletion, process-kill tests                      | M5          | Open   |
| R-004 | Watcher event storms create duplicates or process unstable downloads        | High       | High   | ADR-016 durable per-scope debounce, bounded snapshot/open-handle stability gate, atomic identity-based reconciliation and restart/rename fixtures pass. Migration 0018 persists a monotonic `file_delta`/`full_scope` intent: ambiguity can promote work but never demote required recovery, and an all-scope request is one `BEGIN IMMEDIATE` transaction so partial promotion cannot commit. Exact `notify 8.2.0` feeds a 256-item non-blocking queue; at most 64 signals drain per cycle, while watch-set changes, overflow, rescan, source failure, unmatched routing, multiple paths, create/delete/rename/folder hints, hard links and non-Unix targets force durable full-scope reconciliation. On Unix, an ordinary existing single-link regular-file hint may use the narrow metadata delta only after exact root/parent/leaf identity binding, two matching descriptor-anchored snapshots, no active/interrupted scan and an atomic publication CAS; held handles remain live through publication. The original event age caps the wake and forces root-only metadata reconciliation at five minutes, so sustained writes cannot starve a scope. A recovery signal during an old multi-batch scan persists until a fresh post-signal root scan starts. Direct ignored observations cannot cancel unrelated stabilizing work, explicit transitions merge into bounded operational aggregates, and hint/scanner paths share temp-suffix exclusion. macOS arm64 live create/modify/rename/delete previously passed outside the restricted sandbox; deterministic delta/fallback/temp→final fixtures pass but the expanded live host recheck is pending after external execution quota denial. Windows/Linux/macOS Intel runtime, real kernel overflow/root replacement and load storms remain | M6 | Mitigating |
| R-005 | OCR/image/Office/PDF input causes resource exhaustion or code execution     | Medium     | High   | Controlled-source text/PDF/Office/image boundaries pass. ADR-024's path-free macOS and Windows PNG/JPEG providers enforce source/dimension/pixel/output/observation/chunk/deadline limits, exact pre-publication byte reread, complete-only spatial provenance, and one process-wide native execution slot with bounded first-waiter reservation. The macOS evidence runner adds strict private manifest, digest/identity, 32 MiB case, 512 MiB corpus and one-hour run caps while reusing production image/OCR validation; held directory descriptors, `O_NOFOLLOW_ANY`, relative `openat`, output-parent identity revalidation and `RENAME_EXCL` fixtures fail closed on asset symlinks and directory replacement. Windows additionally owns bounded bytes, rejects rotated/non-finite source coordinates, stores absent confidence honestly, waits for terminal status before close, bounds caller return, and transfers the slot to at most one draining worker. Host policy/state-machine and runner adversarial tests pass; broader concurrent stress/native platform tests, actual Windows/MSIX OCR/cancel/cleanup, representative corpus, Intel/fallback runtime and 8 GB evidence remain | M2          | Mitigating |
| R-006 | Optional AI becomes a hidden hard dependency                                | Medium     | High   | Deterministic fallback as a release gate; providers lazy/unloadable; no key/model in core E2E                                  | M0/M3/M9    | Open   |
| R-007 | 8 GB devices exceed memory/CPU/thermal budgets                              | High       | High   | Bounded queues and extraction caps exist; native OCR is single-slot per process, and exact TOCTOU protection retains at most one additional 32 MiB encoded snapshot plus one bounded reread. Explicit duplicate comparison uses two fixed 64 KiB buffers, a 64 MiB/file cap and cooperative five-second deadline. Native Watch callbacks are non-blocking with a 256-event cap; the coordinator drains 64 signals, scans in 256-entry batches, schedules four due scopes and runs one reconciliation batch per cycle. Temporary ignored-event operational state is bounded per scope/reason by transactional aggregation. The five-minute full-scope safety path and native storm CPU/RSS/thermal behavior are not benchmarked. Cross-process OCR contention, one-provider residency, low-memory mode, peak RSS, and 10k/100k reports remain required | M2/M3/M4/M6/M9 | Open   |
| R-008 | Inferred relations are opaque or cause unsafe confidence                    | Medium     | High   | ADR-018/ADR-019 expose immutable root evidence and exact-root feedback; ADR-020/ADR-021 bind exact-byte relations and reverified correction; ADR-022 permits only explicit numeric filename-version evidence at 9000 basis points and rejects timestamp/size/content/`final` guesses; ADR-023 binds user correction to equivalent directional evidence so renamed/reversed evidence returns to `suggested`. | R-027 | Smart Cleanup false positive or unrecoverable system-trash move causes data loss | High | High | Candidate sources are limited to current exact duplicates, evidence-backed older versions and M2-provenance-backed explainable screenshot groups; groups never prove disposability, and the user selects the keeper and every item. Confirmation binds an immutable candidate/keeper/evidence/source identity/hash/bytes snapshot; any change makes it stale. Every action requires Preview, Policy Validation, immediate identity revalidation, a per-file durable journal, no-overwrite platform-trash execution, exact receipt/identity verification, crash recovery and idempotent Undo while the exact item exists. Trash is an action-bound capability and cannot become a scope or be enumerated/searched/exposed. Rules, notifications, LLMs, batches and MCP cannot bypass confirmation. Batches are capped at 100 items/100 GiB, stop on the first non-completed item and preserve per-item outcomes/Undo without batch rollback. Permanent delete and empty-trash capabilities do not exist.
